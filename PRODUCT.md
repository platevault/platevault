# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Astrophotographers with large local libraries who need to manage acquisition
sessions, calibration reuse, processing projects, target history, generated
source views, manifests, and cleanup/archive decisions across multiple tools.
They use the app at a desk alongside filesystem tools, capture software exports,
PixInsight/WBPP, and planetary or lunar processing tools.

## Product Purpose

Astro Library Manager organizes and documents an astrophotography library
without replacing processing tools. It makes source data, calibration
relationships, target coverage, app-owned project structure, project lifecycle,
and filesystem plans explicit, reviewable, and auditable. Success means users
can understand what data they have, prepare tool-friendly project views, and
clean/archive safely without guessing or mutating files silently.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, technical, and safety-first. The interface should feel like an
expert workbench for source-of-truth decisions, not a marketing dashboard or a
decorative astronomy showcase.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style SaaS dashboards, decorative identical card grids, dark
blue admin templates, flashy astronomy theming, gradient hero treatments, and
interfaces that make filesystem risk feel casual. Do not bury destructive or
cleanup-related decisions behind vague controls.

## Design Principles

- Make source truth visible: separate observed files, inferred relationships,
  reviewed decisions, and generated projections.
- Put safety before speed: every filesystem mutation, cleanup, archive, or link
  removal path must feel deliberate and auditable.
- Keep lifecycle contextual: project and target lifecycle belong primarily in
  project and target workflows, with a global sweep surface for cross-library
  cleanup/archive review.
- Support expert density without visual noise: prioritize scanning,
  comparison, filtering, and repeated review over presentation.
- Stay tool-agnostic: PixInsight, planetary/lunar tools, and future workflows
  are selectable profiles, not hardcoded assumptions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Support keyboard-first navigation for review workflows, visible
focus states, reduced-motion operation, semantic status/error messaging, and
clear differentiation that does not rely on color alone.
